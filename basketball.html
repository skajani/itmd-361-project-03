Top 5 Most Inspirational Basketball Players

Michael Jeffrey Jordan

Micheal Jordan changed the way people thought the way basketball can be played. He has inspired countless generations to not only play basketball but any sport and show that hard work really shows.

Quotes:

  • "Some people want it to happen, some wish it would happen, others make it happen."
  • "Never say never, because limits, like fears, are often just an illusion."
  • "You must expect great things of yourself before you can do them."

Kobe Bean Bryant

Kobe Bryant is beyond inspirational. He inspired teammates, family, and athletes around the world to become the best version of who they can be. Even after his passing, he inspires people to strive for greatness.

Quotes:

  • "The most important thing is to try and inspire people so that they can be great in whatever they want to do."
  • "Everything negative - pressure, challenges - is all an opportunity for me to rise."
  • "I can't relate to lazy people."

Lebron Raymone James

Contending for one of the greatest to ever play the game, Lebron James has made a difference on and off the court, changing the lives of many and inspiring people beyond just the basketball court.

Quotes:

  • "You can't be afraid to fail. It's the only way you succeed - you're not gonna succeed all the time, and I know that."
  • "You have to be able to accept failure to get better."
  • "I think, team first. It allows me to succeed, it allows my team to succeed."

Wardell Stephen Curry

Another man who has changed the way the game is played. Although most people may not put him in this list, I think he has changed the game. Through excellent perimeter shooting, he inspired countless athletes to shoot deeper shots and believe in themselves.

Quotes:

  • "Success is not an accident, success is actually a choice."
  • "Success is born out of faith, an undying passion, and a relentless drive."
  • "What I tell people is be the best version of yourself in anything that you do."

Derrick Martell Rose

Maybe a suprising pick but being the youngest MVP (Most Valuable Player), Derrick Rose showed that age does not matter. If you give it your all and work hard, anything is possible even as high of an award as MVP at only 22 years old.

Quotes:

  • "All the days that you wake up you have one job and that's to get better, every single day."
  • "Hard work isn't enough. You got to believe."
  • "Life has 2 rules:
    1. Never Quit
    2. Always Remember Rule #1"
